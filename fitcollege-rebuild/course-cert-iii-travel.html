Certificate III in Travel

Overview

SIT30222 Certificate III in Travel is a nationally recognised qualification for anyone starting a career in travel — whether you want to work in an agency or run your own home-based travel business. As travel becomes more complex, demand for qualified advisors who can navigate it is rising.

What you'll learn

  • The travel & tourism industry
  • Work health & safety
  • Customer service
  • Selling travel — products, Australian & international destinations, sales techniques
  • Booking procedures — quotes, supplier bookings, documentation
  • Travel reservations — Amadeus GDS and PNR management
  • Domestic airfares
  • International airfares — fare construction and IATA standards

Where it takes you

Roles as a Travel Advisor, Personal Travel Consultant, Itinerary Designer, Luxury Travel Planner or Corporate Consultant — or join a host travel agency. It also opens a pathway to the Certificate IV in Travel & Tourism and tourism/hospitality degrees.

Questions

Certificate III in Travel FAQ

Do I need experience to start?

No — the course starts from the fundamentals of the travel and tourism industry and builds to airfare construction and reservations.

Is it nationally recognised?

Yes — SIT30222 Certificate III in Travel is a nationally recognised qualification. On completion you receive the qualification and an academic transcript.

Can I study while working?

Yes — it's 100% online and self-paced, so you fit study around your schedule.
