Overview

FIT Elite™ is FIT College's dual-career personal trainer program combining the SIS30321 Certificate III in Fitness and SIS40221 Certificate IV in Fitness with a CEC short course pack and a choice of specialty stream: the ASCA Level 1-accredited Strength & Conditioning stream or the CHCSS00130 Disability Skill Set. New PTs don't fail because they're unqualified — they fail because a client book takes months to build. FIT Elite graduates with the disability stream can take paid disability support work from week one, alongside every hour they invest in growing their PT business.

What you'll learn

  • SIS30321 Certificate III in Fitness — the gym instructor qualification
  • SIS40221 Certificate IV in Fitness — the personal trainer qualification
  • 30-CEC short course pack: kettlebell, boxing and suspension training
  • Your specialty stream: CHCSS00130 Disability Skill Set or ASCA Level 1 Strength & Conditioning

Where it takes you

Personal training in gyms, studios and your own business — plus, for disability-stream graduates, paid support work in a sector with immediate demand. Our careers liaison team works with graduates and affiliate gyms nationally.

Questions

FIT Elite™ FAQ

Can a personal trainer work in disability support?

Yes. With the CHCSS00130 Disability Skill Set, a qualified personal trainer can also work in disability support roles — a growing sector with immediate demand. FIT Elite graduates on the disability stream qualify for both career paths.

What's the difference between FIT Elite and doing the Cert III and IV separately?

FIT Elite packages both fitness qualifications with a CEC short course pack and a specialty stream in one structured program, so you graduate with a broader scope of practice than the standalone qualifications provide.

Do I need experience to enrol?

No prior industry experience is required. FIT Elite starts with the Certificate III in Fitness, the entry-level qualification, and builds from there.
