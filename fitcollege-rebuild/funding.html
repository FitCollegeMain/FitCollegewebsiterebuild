Questions

Funding FAQ

What is the Career Start programme in Queensland?

Career Start is a Queensland Government subsidy programme (effective 1 July 2025, replacing the Certificate 3 Guarantee) that funds eligible Queenslanders to complete their first Certificate III qualification with an approved Skills Assure Supplier.

Who is eligible for Career Start funding?

In general: Queensland residents who have finished school and have not previously completed a Certificate III or higher qualification. Eligibility is confirmed at enrolment against Queensland Government criteria.

How much does a personal trainer course cost in QLD with government funding?

Eligible students pay a co-contribution for the SIS30321 Certificate III in Fitness: $1,750 (concessional) or $2,000 (non-concessional). The Queensland Government funds the balance. Figures current as at July 2026.

What happened to the Certificate 3 Guarantee?

It was replaced by the Career Start programme from 1 July 2025. FIT College is an approved Skills Assure Supplier under Career Start.

What is a Skills Assure Supplier?

A training provider approved by the Queensland Government to deliver subsidised training under its funding programmes. FIT College holds Skills Assure Supplier status (Agreement QS102935).

Can I use Career Start for the Cert IV in Fitness?

Career Start covers the SIS30321 Certificate III in Fitness. Many students complete the funded Cert III and continue to the Cert IV — a Career Advisor can map the pathway and payment options.

I'm over 40 — what funding can I access?

Skills Checkpoint funding of up to $2,200 may be available for eligible people aged 40 and over. Check your eligibility or ask a Career Advisor.
