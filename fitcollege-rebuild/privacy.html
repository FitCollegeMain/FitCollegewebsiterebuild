Privacy Policy

How FIT College Pty Ltd (RTO 31903) collects, uses, discloses and protects your personal information.

[CONFIRM — legal master] This page mirrors the section structure of the current live policy. Paste the approved verbatim legal copy from the FIT College legal master before publishing; the summaries below are placeholders for layout only.

Introduction

FIT College Pty Ltd is committed to collecting and protecting personal information in accordance with the GDPR and Australian privacy laws, including the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

Scope and purpose

This policy applies to FIT College's data-processing activities within Australia and is designed to ensure compliance with the GDPR and relevant data-protection legislation.

Definitions

Defines key terms including GDPR, Data Controller, Data Subject, Personal Information and Sensitive Personal Information under the relevant legislation.

Principles of data processing

FIT College commits to seven core principles: lawfulness and transparency, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality, and accountability.

Data subject rights

Individuals have rights to access, rectification, erasure (under conditions), restriction of processing, data portability, objection, and exemption from solely automated decision-making.

Data we collect

Identification data, demographic information, health and fitness data, payment details and technical data necessary for service provision and legal compliance — including USI numbers for VET students.

How we use your data

Personal information is used for service delivery and legal compliance. Third-party disclosure requires consent unless legally mandated, with specific provisions for student-identifier verification and AQF certification.

Disclosure under the ESOS Act

Information may be shared with government agencies, the Tuition Protection Service and authorities under the Education Services for Overseas Students Act 2000 for compliance and student-welfare purposes.

Sharing of personal information and correspondence

Data may be shared with related entities for service improvement and marketing. You can opt out of communications at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions.

Consent and withdrawal

Consent must be freely given and informed. You may withdraw consent at any time, as easily as it was given, and opt out of direct marketing.

Data security measures

Encryption, secure protocols, access limitations, regular testing and breach-response procedures protect the integrity and confidentiality of your personal data.

Data breach notification

Breaches are reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours, and to affected individuals without undue delay where there is a high risk to their rights and freedoms.

International data transfers

Transfers outside Australia or the EEA occur only with adequate protection assurances, appropriate safeguards, or explicit informed consent.

Organisational responsibilities

A Data Protection Officer oversees compliance, monitoring and audits, and is the contact point for data subjects and authorities. Contact: Suite 8, 102 Wises Road, Maroochydore. [CONFIRM — from GBP NAP Master]

Critical incident policy

FIT College maintains documented procedures for traumatic events affecting students or staff, including immediate response, follow-up and documentation, adhering to privacy principles.

Policy updates

This policy may be updated. Significant changes are communicated via email or the website with a revised date.

Complaints and enquiries

Complaints should be directed to FIT College's head office. Unresolved issues can be escalated to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

Governing law and jurisdiction

Australian law governs this policy, with disputes resolved through Australian courts unless jurisdiction-specific laws apply.
